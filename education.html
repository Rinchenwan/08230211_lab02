My Education

Click to View My Education

Kupiyalsa Primary School is where I studied from class PP till class four. It holds my biggest childhood memories. I had to walk for 20 minutes to reach the school, as there was no boarding facility at all. Whether it was raining or sunny, I had to walk every single day(PP–4)

Wambur Primary School was the place where my life changed because it was my first experience living in a boarding school. At first, I had no idea how to be independent on my own, but somehow, I managed. If I ever got a chance to go back, I would probably choose this school because it taught me so many life lessons. Even though it was in a remote place, it was peaceful and full of memories that shaped me.(4–6)

Autsho Central School was where I stepped into adulthood. It was here that I learned to handle everything on my own — from friendship betrayals and heartbreaks to procrastinating on homework. It was kind of nice in a way because these challenges helped me grow and become stronger. I also met some of my closest friends there, friends who are still with me today.(7–10)

Lhuntse Higher Secondary School was where I became a pro at skipping classes and not listening to teachers. At first, I wanted to take science, but unfortunately, I got placed in the arts stream. From the moment I became an arts student, my dream of becoming who I wanted to be slowly faded. I finished high school with many traumas and lessons learned along the way. (11–12)

Samtse College of Education (B.Ed IT, 3rd Year) Samtse College of Education felt like a blessing — I believe God heard my prayers when I got selected to study there. At first, I was happy, but then I found out my course was STEM-based, which was completely new to me since I had never studied it before. It was difficult at the beginning, but as time went on, I learned so much from my classmates and peers. I am truly thankful to everyone who kept motivating me and helped me whenever I needed support. There is still more to come in this journey. This is my educational life so far — my future is unknown, but I’m ready to keep going
